Torque-Slip Characteristics of Induction Motors

Topic Overview: This section explores how a 3-phase induction motor behaves under varying loads. The Torque-Slip curve is the primary tool used to determine starting torque, pull-out torque, and the stable operating range of the motor.

Q1. Consider the following statements regarding torque–slip characteristics:

1. Maximum torque is independent of rotor resistance.
2. Maximum torque is proportional to square of applied voltage.
3. At starting condition, slip is zero.
4. Increasing rotor resistance shifts maximum torque towards higher slip.

Which statements are correct?

A) 1 and 2 only
B) 1, 2 and 4 only
C) 2 and 3 only
D) 1, 3 and 4 only
Detailed Explanation:
Statement 3 is the key error here. At the moment of starting, the rotor speed is 0, which means the slip is at its maximum value of 1. Statements 1, 2, and 4 are standard physical laws of induction machines.
Correct Answer: Option B (1, 2 and 4 only)

Solution Analysis

The general Torque Equation is expressed as:

Torque = (3 ÷ Synchronous Speed) × (Slip × E2 squared × R2) ÷ (R2 squared + (Slip × X2) squared)

✔ Statement 1: Maximum torque (Tmax) formula shows it depends on reactance and voltage, but is independent of R2.

✔ Statement 2: Since Torque is proportional to (Voltage squared), doubling the voltage quadruples the torque.

❌ Statement 3: Slip = (Synchronous Speed - Rotor Speed) ÷ Synchronous Speed. At start, Rotor Speed = 0, so Slip = 1.

✔ Statement 4: Resistance shifts the "peak" of the curve but not the height of the peak.

Nyquist Stability Criterion

Topic Overview: The Nyquist Criterion relates the path of the open-loop frequency response to the number of poles and zeros in the right-half of the s-plane. It is used to ensure system stability without needing to solve for the exact roots of the characteristic equation.

Q3. Consider the following statements regarding Nyquist plot:

1. Nyquist plot determines closed-loop stability using open-loop response.
2. Stability depends on encirclements of (-1, 0).
3. Nyquist criterion applies only to minimum phase systems.
4. Encirclements depend on number of open-loop RHP poles.

A) 1 and 2 only
B) 1, 2 and 4 only
C) 2 and 3 only
D) 1, 3 and 4 only
Detailed Explanation:
Statement 3 is incorrect because the Nyquist Criterion is powerful enough to handle both minimum and non-minimum phase systems, including those that are inherently unstable in open-loop.
Correct Answer: Option B (1, 2 and 4 only)

Solution Analysis

The stability equation is defined as:

N = Z - P

Where:
N = Number of clockwise encirclements of (-1, 0)
Z = Number of closed-loop poles in Right Half Plane
P = Number of open-loop poles in Right Half Plane

✔ Statement 1: It maps the open-loop transfer function G(s)H(s).

✔ Statement 2: The point (-1, 0) is the critical boundary for stability.

❌ Statement 3: It is universal and works for all system types.

✔ Statement 4: The encirclements are compared against P to find Z.
