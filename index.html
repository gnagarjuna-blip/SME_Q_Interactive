Q1. Torque–Slip Characteristics of Induction Motors

Topic Overview: Before analyzing the specific statements, it is vital to understand that the torque in an induction motor is a function of slip, voltage, and rotor impedance. The behavior changes significantly from the starting condition (standstill) to full-speed operation.

Consider the following statements:

  1. Maximum torque is independent of rotor resistance.
  2. Maximum torque is proportional to square of applied voltage.
  3. At starting condition, slip is zero.
  4. Increasing rotor resistance shifts maximum torque towards higher slip.
Option A: 1 and 2 only
This selection recognizes the independence of peak torque from resistance and its relationship with voltage, but it fails to account for the crucial Statement 4 regarding slip shifting.
Option B: 1, 2 and 4 only
The complete accurate set. It identifies that while resistance doesn't change the peak torque value, it does determine the speed at which that peak occurs.
Option C: 2 and 3 only
Incorrect. This option relies on Statement 3, which is a physical impossibility for an induction motor at starting.
Option D: 1, 3 and 4 only
Incorrect. Despite including Statement 4, the inclusion of Statement 3 makes the entire selection false.

Comprehensive Solution & Theory

The performance of an induction motor is governed by the torque equation. To maximize starting torque, external resistance is often added to the rotor circuit, but this does not increase the maximum possible torque the motor can produce.

Torque = (3 ÷ ωs) × (s E22 R2) ÷ (R22 + (s X2)2)

Step-by-Step Analysis:

✔ Statement 1: Maximum torque Tmax = (3 ÷ 2ωs) × (E22 ÷ X2). Notice R2 is not in the numerator or denominator of the peak value.

✔ Statement 2: Torque is proportional to E22. Since E2 is proportional to Supply Voltage V, Torque is proportional to V2.

❌ Statement 3: Slip s = (Ns - Nr) ÷ Ns. At start, Nr = 0, so s = 1. Slip is maximum, not zero.

✔ Statement 4: The slip at maximum torque sm = R2 ÷ X2. Thus, increasing R2 directly increases sm.
